Every morning I follow the same ritual. At 7:40 am, the
radio alarm
goes off and I awake blurry eyed and wanting to sleep more - I have never been a morning person. I remove the feather
duvet
that keeps me warm during the night and look in envy as my wife continues to sleep. I reach for my
glasses
that are on the
dressing table
and, after a quick visit to the bathroom, cross the hall to my son's bedroom. I sit on the
stool
next to his
cot
and watch him for a few moments as he sleeps peacefully, with out a care in the world. As soon as the
quilt
begins to move I know he is about to wake up. He is famous for being noisy in the morning so I have to be ready to cuddle him and prevent him from waking up the rest of the family; after all, a tired wife is a scary wife.
